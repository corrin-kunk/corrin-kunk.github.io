Pacific Rhododendron

Scientific Name:Rhododendron macrophyllum D. Don ex G. Don

Common Alternate Names of the Plant:

  • California Rosebay
  • California Rhododendron
  • Coast Rhododendron
  • Bigleaf Rhododendron

Size

Pacific Rhododendron is a large shrub that can grow up to 26 feet tall. It has large, leathery leaves that can be up to 3-9 inches long and 1-3 inches wide. Each flower measures about 1-1.5 inches long.

Geographic Range

Pacific Rhododendron is native to the western coast of North America, specifically from British Columbia in Canada down to California in the United States. It is commonly found in coastal regions, particularly in areas with moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

Climate

Pacific Rhododendron thrives in a temperate climate with mild, wet winters and cool, dry summers. It prefers areas with high humidity and consistent moisture levels, making it well-suited to coastal environments.
